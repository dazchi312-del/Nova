under open source and open content licenses (see `LICENSE`, `LICENSE-DOCS`,
and `LICENSE-SCHEMAS`). **Those licenses cover copyright. They do not cover
trademarks.**

This document describes the marks associated with the Nova project, the
provenance of those marks, and the terms under which they may, and may not,
be used.

The marks described here are actively used, actively maintained, and will
be actively enforced.

## Provenance of the Marks

The origin and authorship of these marks is part of the public record of
this project.

### The bee-and-lightning seal

The seal is derived from an original mixed-media painting created by the
author using charcoal and oil paint. The painting predates this software
project and is the original creative work of the author. The seal therefore
carries **two overlapping protections**:

- **Copyright** in the underlying artwork, held by the author from the
  moment of creation.
- **Trademark** in its use as a source identifier for the Nova
  Imagination Engine project.

Reproduction of the seal — in any medium, any color, any stylization —
requires permission under either or both bodies of law.

### The "Dazchicago" mark

"Dazchicago" is a coined term constructed from the author's initials
combined with the author's home city of Chicago. It is not a descriptive
term, not a dictionary word, and has no prior use in commerce by any other
party in connection with software, artificial intelligence, or related
fields. The author asserts originating use of this mark.

### The "Nova Imagination Engine" mark

The phrase "Nova Imagination Engine" was coined by the author as the name
of this project and has been used continuously in public distribution
since the project's first public commit. While the word "Nova" alone is
common, the full phrase and its use within the domain of sovereign AI
tooling are distinctive to this project.

## What You May Do

You **may**, without asking permission:

- **Reference the project by name** in articles, blog posts, academic
  papers, videos, social media, and other commentary. ("I tried Nova
  Imagination Engine and here's what I found.")
- **Link to this repository** using the project name.
- **State that your project uses, integrates with, or is compatible with
  Nova** — provided the statement is accurate and does not imply
  endorsement or official affiliation.
- **Fork the code** under the terms of the AGPL-3.0 license. Forking the
  code is a copyright matter, not a trademark matter. You can fork freely.

## What You May Not Do

You **may not**, without prior written permission:

- **Name a fork, derivative, product, or service "Nova," "Nova Imagination
  Engine," "Dazchicago," or any confusingly similar variant.** If you fork
  this project and distribute it, you must rename it. ("Stella Imagination
  Engine, based on Nova" is acceptable. "Nova Pro" is not.)
- **Reproduce, adapt, or display the bee-and-lightning seal** on your own
  forks, products, websites, merchandise, marketing materials, social
  media avatars, or any other public-facing surface. The seal is both a
  trademark and a copyrighted artwork; both protections apply
  independently.
- **Imply endorsement, sponsorship, partnership, or affiliation** with the
  Nova project, Dazchicago, or the author, where none exists.
- **Register domain names, social media handles, package names, app store
  listings, or trademarks** that incorporate "Nova," "Nova Imagination
  Engine," or "Dazchicago" in a way likely to cause confusion with this
  project.
- **Use these marks in connection with cryptocurrency tokens, NFTs,
  blockchain projects, scams, spam, harassment, or any deceptive or
  unlawful activity.** This use is categorically prohibited.

## Why These Restrictions Exist

The AGPL-3.0 license guarantees the freedom to study, modify, and
redistribute the *code*. Trademark law guarantees that when someone
encounters the name "Nova" or the bee-and-lightning seal, they can trust
they are looking at work from the original project — not a fork,
repackage, or impersonation.

These two protections work together. The code is free. The identity is
not.

This is the same approach taken by Mozilla (Firefox), the Linux Foundation
(Linux), the Python Software Foundation (Python), Automattic (WordPress),
and many other open source projects. Free code. Protected name.

## Enforcement

The author actively monitors use of these marks and will enforce these
rights. Enforcement may include, but is not limited to: takedown requests
to hosting providers and platforms (GitHub, package registries, social
media, app stores), DMCA notices where copyright in the seal artwork is
also infringed, domain dispute proceedings, and where warranted, legal
action.

Most issues are resolved informally and amicably. The author's strong
preference is a polite email or issue resulting in a rename or removal.
Formal enforcement is reserved for bad-faith actors, impersonation, and
commercial misuse.

## Requesting Permission

If you want to use one of the protected marks in a way not covered above —
for example, an article series, a conference talk, merchandise, an
academic study, a podcast episode, or a commercial integration — please
open an issue in this repository titled `Trademark Request: <your use
case>` or contact the maintainer directly.

Reasonable requests for non-commercial, accurate, non-confusing use will
generally be granted in writing. The goal is not to suppress discussion.
The goal is to prevent impersonation, dilution, and commercial freeriding.

## Forks and Renaming Guidance

If you fork this project under the AGPL-3.0 and intend to distribute your
fork:

1. **Choose a new name** that does not include "Nova," "Imagination
   Engine," or "Dazchicago."
2. **Remove the bee-and-lightning seal** from all user-facing materials
   in your fork. Do not adapt it, recolor it, or create a derivative
   version. Replace it with your own original mark.
3. **You may state**, in your README and documentation: *"This project is
   a fork of Nova Imagination Engine
   (https://github.com/<this-repo>), originally created by Dazchicago."*
   This is accurate attribution and is encouraged.
4. **You may not state** that your fork is "Nova," "the new Nova," "Nova
   2.0," or otherwise imply continuity with the original project's
   identity.
