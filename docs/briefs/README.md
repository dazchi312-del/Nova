# Educational Briefs

Narrative companions to Nova's architectural work. Where ADRs record
*decisions* (terse, formal, "we chose X because Y"), briefs record
*understanding* (pedagogical, "here's what we built, why it matters,
and where it leads").

## Purpose

- **Capture the click.** When a piece of the system finally makes sense,
  the brief preserves that moment of understanding for future review.
- **Connect the dots.** Briefs explain how a change fits into the larger
  arc — what came before, what it enables next.
- **Teach future-self.** Re-reading a brief months later lets you measure
  how your model of the system has deepened against the same text.

## Relationship to ADRs

| | ADR | Brief |
|---|---|---|
| **Question** | Why did we decide this? | What did we build and what does it mean? |
| **Tone** | Formal, terse | Narrative, pedagogical |
| **Anchor** | A decision | A milestone of understanding |
| **Audience** | Future maintainers evaluating the choice | Future-self (and collaborators) building intuition |

A brief often *references* one or more ADRs. ADRs rarely reference briefs.
